Matt's microtonal calculator

What is this?

This page is my attempt at making a general purpose calculator/converter for microtonal music. If you have suggestions for other functionality I should add, send me an email!

At the moment, this calculator can:

  • Convert between cents (e.g. 400c), just ratios (e.g. 5/4), EDO steps (e.g. 4\12), intervals in FJS notation (e.g. M3^5), and intervals in ups-and-downs notation (e.g. vM3\22).
  • Compute multiplication, division, and exponentiation of just ratios (e.g. 80/81 * (9/8)^2) as well as intervals in EDO step, FJS, and ups-and-downs notation (e.g. 3/2 / 4\12, (M3^5)^2, vM3\22 * m2_5).
  • Compute addition, subtraction, and scaling of cents values as well as intervals in EDO step, FJS, and ups-and-downs notation (e.g. P1^5 + 2 x 100c, 700c - 4\12, (M3^5) x 2, vM3\22 + m2_5).
  • List best rational approximations and best EDO approximations of an interval.
  • Do analogous versions of the above with notes:
    • Convert between hertz (e.g. 370Hz), notes in FJS notation (e.g. F‚ôØ^5), and notes in ups-and-downs notation (e.g. vF‚ôØ\22).
    • Compute multiplication of a note in hertz, FJS, or ups-and-downs notation by a just interval or an interval in EDO step, FJS, or ups-and-downs notation (e.g. C4 * 5/4, Ab\22 * M2^5).
    • Compute addition of a note in FJS or ups-and-downs notation with a cents value or an interval in EDO step, FJS, or ups-and-downs notation (e.g. C4 + 400c, Ab\22 + M2^5).
    • Take the ratio of two notes in hertz, FJS, or ups-and-downs notation to get an interval (e.g. E4^5 / C4) or subtract two notes in FJS or ups-and-downs notation to get an interval.
  • Set a reference note and tuning (e.g. C4 where A = 432hz) or set a reference note with automatically chosen tuning (e.g. F‚ôØ\12 / D where D = D\12)
  • Compute a number of useful functions:
    • Octave-reduce or balanced octave-reduce any interval (e.g. red(7/3), reb(16/9)).
    • Reduce or balanced reduce any interval by any other interval (e.g. red(M6, 3/2)).
    • Take the mediant of two just intervals (e.g. med(5/4, 9/7)).
    • Find the best approximation of an interval in a given EDO (e.g. approx(5/4, 41)).
    • Convert a just interval to its value in cents (e.g. cents(5/4) + 100c).
